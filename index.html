About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Sociology Department at SUNY Albany. My research interests include migration, globalization, youth culture, race and ethnicity, citizenship, sociological theory, and qualitative research methods. My Ph.D. dissertation investigates inequality in transnational youth mobilities based on visa status, class, education, gender, race, age, sexual orientation, and nationality. The target population comprises Chinese youths who are currently in or have been in Australia using a working holiday visa. By discussing cosmopolitanism, nationalism, racism, morality, reflexivity, and humanity, my research aims to contribute to a better understanding of cultural identity, integration, and theoretical issues surrounding assimilation, acculturation, multiculturalism, and cultural preservation in the context of globalization and transnational movements.
